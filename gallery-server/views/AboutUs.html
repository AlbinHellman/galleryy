How did we start this Business?

Read the story behind The Worlds Artist. In 2006 after I retired, I started to concentrate my time doing what I always loved to do, my art related projects. I met a very skillful painter in LA, who was doing a fine art reproduction of a picture hanging in the museum, and an idea came to mind. A partnership formed and a business is started. Now, twelve years later, The World Artist employs many talented painters and friends who only do hand-painted oil painting reproductions on canvas and portraits from photographs.

What's the Then & Now Photography and Tilt-Shift Photo?

For fun and a little art history, we created the very interesting Then and Now, plus Tilt-Shift painting photos. See, the present day location that inspired the famous artists. We travel the world to find the exact location where the artists set up their easel to paint. We photograph it as it appears today. Now, you can compare side by side photos of the original oil paintings and the present-day locations. You can see what beautiful or interesting thing they saw that captured their imagination enough for them to paint it.

Can a renowned oil painting be re-interpreted in a new method? Using museum oil paintings we decided to try to see what would happen. We took some of the worlds best oil paintings from the most famous painters and using Tilt-Shift Photography and selective focus, have made a fantastic new interpretation of some of their classic artwork.

Nothing in any of these oil paintings been added or removed, the results range from a look of miniaturization to a look of 3-D, as you will see. We are sure you will discover several paintings as you have never seen them before. Take a fresh look at Tilt-Shift paintings by many painters including: Achenbach, Bierstadt, Constable, El Greco, Guillaumin, Picabia, Turner and of course Tilt-Shift Van Gogh. See them all in our THEN & NOW art section.

How Renoir Painted Oil Paintings and Portraits?

We paint the same way that Rembrandt and Renoir painted. We use paint brushes, oil paints, canvas, an artist palette and a mahl stick. That's it, he painted that way and we paint it that way, 100% painted by hand on canvas. Many of the renowned painters as part of their artistic training, were required to go to the museums and reproduce famous oil paintings on location. If they liked a particular artist or they wanted to paint in the same artistic style, then in addition to their Academy training, the art museum was the place to go. You can read in our art movements or artist biography section about all the artists that started off doing what we are doing, making oil painting reproductions of famous art masterpieces.
